Education

  1. Khan Academy

Intro: Khan Academy, an educational non-profit founded by Bangladeshi-American Salman Khan, aims to use online films for free lectures on math, history, finance, physics, chemistry, biology, astronomy and more than 2,000 segments, with the agency’s mission to speed up learning for students of all ages.

Khan Academy, an online library with more than 3,500 videos of Khan teachers teaching, provides free, high-quality education to people around the world. The project, which began with an online video lesson taught by Salman Khan to relatives’ children, quickly spread around and from home into school, even “flipping the classroom”, which is thought to be opening the way for “education of the future.” Founder Khan, whose full name is Salman Khan, is of Bangladeshi descent, has excelled in his junior studies, studied mathematics and electrical computer engineering at MIT, studied mathematics and electrical computer engineering at the university, and after his work took an MBA at Harvard, where he taught everything from math, physics, chemistry to financial life, but he never appeared in front of the camera himself.

  1. Coursera

Intro: Coursera is a large open online program founded by two computer science professors at Stanford University in the United States. Designed to partner with the world’s top universities to offer online open courses. Coursera’s first partner institutions include Stanford University, the University of Michigan, Princeton University, the University of Pennsylvania and other prestigious U.S. universities.

Coursera has a cooperation agreement with another. More than 1.5 million students enrolled in its courses come from more than 190 countries and regions around the world, compared with 680,000 students enrolled on the website. Sign up for 124 courses. Current additions include Georgia Institute of Technology, Duke University, University of Washington, Caltech, Rice University, University of Edinburgh, University of Toronto, Lausanne Federal Institute of Technology - Lausanne (Switzerland), Johns Hopkins University School of Public Health, University of California, San Francisco, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, and University of Virginia.

  1. edX

Intro: edX is a large-scale open online classroom platform created by MIT and Harvard University in April 2012. It provides free online classes at the level of university education to the general public. The two universities each funded $30 million in the nonprofit program. In the fall of 2012, edX was launched in MITx.

  1. TED

Intro: TED is a privately owned non-profit organization in the United States known for its TED Conference, which aims to “spread all the ideas worth spreading.” TED was born in 1984 by Richard So Uman. Since 2001, Chris Anderson has taken over TED, founded the Seed Foundation and run the TED Conference. Every March, the TED Conference brings together a wide range of prominent figures in science, design, literature, and music in North America to share their thoughts and explorations on technology, society, and people.

  1. Udacity

Intro: Founded in 2011 by Sebastian Thrun, David Stavens, and Mike Sokolsky, Udacity is an online educational platform that offers MOOCs (massive open online courses).

  1. Udemy

Intro: The name Udemy has its special meaning. “Udemy” is synthesized by “you” plus “academy”, meaning “your university”. Unlike other online educational platforms, Udemy is not only open to courses, but also open to teaching opportunities. In other words, you can become a teacher if you want to. Users can take any course on the platform at any time, or they can build their own, introducing their expertise and knowledge to the world at the discretion of the teacher, usually ranging from $20 to $100. In addition to the web version, Udemy also has iOS and Android apps that let users learn at their own pace, regardless of time, place, and device.

  1. Bilibili

Intro: Most educational videos can be found on Bilibili for free, but some of them are without permissions. It’s kind of you to watch the original, if there is no permission.
